# User Journey: GauchoGuide

## User Goal / Need
The user wants to make informed decisions about which UCSB classes to take by easily understanding course content, workload, and student experiences without having to search across many disconnected sources.

---

## Beginning: Problem Awareness
- The user realizes they need to choose classes for an upcoming quarter.
- They feel uncertain because existing tools provide limited or fragmented information.
- They want confidence that their schedule aligns with their academic goals, interests, and capacity.

---

## Middle: Interaction with GauchoGuide
- The user seeks out GauchoGuide as a centralized source for class-related information.
- They explore summaries of courses that compile data from multiple sources.
- The user reviews high-level insights about classes, such as themes, expectations, and commonly mentioned challenges or benefits.
- They compare multiple classes to understand trade-offs and identify which options best fit their needs.

At this stage, GauchoGuide helps reduce cognitive load by organizing and synthesizing information rather than requiring the user to interpret raw data themselves.

---

## End: Value Exchange and Outcome
- The user selects classes with greater confidence and clarity.
- Their original need—to make an informed scheduling decision—is met.
- The user saves time, experiences less stress, and feels more prepared for the upcoming quarter.

In exchange, GauchoGuide fulfills its role as a trusted guide by transforming scattered information into clear, actionable understanding for students.
